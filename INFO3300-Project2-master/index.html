WHERE DOES IT PAY TO ATTEND COLLEGE?

Wondering which college to pick so you can earn those big bucks 💰 ? Want to know if your SAT score is good enough to get you into one of the "elite" schools 🎓 ? Scratching your head over whether majoring in Philosophy is worth it 🤔 ?

We've all been there. We feel you. That's why we've made this interactive page based on data collected by the Wall Street Journal and PayScale Inc. Picking a college and a major is hard but we hope these charts make those decisions a little bit easier. Good luck! 👍

SALARY INCREASE BY TYPE OF COLLEGE OR MAJOR

Explore salaries by school type Explore salaries by major

Party school? Engineering Institute? State School? You already know your starting salary will be different depending on what type of school you attend. See where your school ranks!

Ten years out, graduates of Ivy League schools earned 99% more than they did at graduation. Party school graduates saw an 85% increase in salary by the middle of their careers. Engineering school graduates did not see such high increases in pay, earning only 76% more 10 years out of school than when they joined the workforce.

The battle is far from over even after you painstakingly settle on a school. Now the question is, "What should I major in?"

Your parents might have worried when you chose Philosophy or International Relations as a major. But do those majors really not earn as much? Do their salaries just start off small but increase later? Or do philosophers consistently earn less than their engineer counterparts? Find out for yourself!

A year-long survey by PayScale, Inc. of 1.2 million people with only a bachelor's degree shows that graduates in philosophy, international relations earned 103.5% and 97.8% more respectively than what they started with. This increase was seen about 10 years post-commencement. Majors that didn't show as much salary growth include Nursing and Information Technology.
